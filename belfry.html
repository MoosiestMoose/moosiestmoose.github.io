16th December - Belfry Trivia ⛪✨⛪


The word belfry comes from the Old North French berfroi or berfrei, meaning "movable wooden siege tower".
The Old French word itself is derived from Middle High German bercfrit, meaning "protecting shelter".

However, some time between 1290 and 1450 it was renamed to, "Belfry". Why?

  1. Overwhelming dislike of the French language by the landowners.
  2. The gentry didn't want the peasants trying to move the towers.
  3. People thought the word had something to do with bells.
  4. Sir Richard Bellmont-Fry built several towers near Winchester.
